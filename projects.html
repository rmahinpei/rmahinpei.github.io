Python Model - Tracking Blood Alcohol Concentration

April 2021 - As part of a final project for my first-year in university, I had to work with a partner and come up with a unique project proposal. Considering my interest in CS and my partner’s interest in biology, we decided to combine the two domains for our project and look at how different alcohol consumption behaviours affect the trend in blood alcohol concentration (BAC) over time.
My amazing partner (Stephanie Lapadat) proposed a simplified compartmental pathway for alcohol within the human body, which I then implemented with Python in Jupyter Notebook in order for us to collect data. I used this free textbook to expand my Python knowledge for this project.

Python Model - Tracking Weight after Calorie Reduction

March 2021 - As a part of UBC’s Research Experience (REX) program, I had the privilege to work under the supervision of Dr. Leah-Edelstein Keshet (UBC Math Department) and start a project with personal significance. Math Publication - Minimizing the Use of Polyethene inside Paper Coffee Cups

March 2021 - It always saddens me to see how the vast applications of mathematics go unappreciated. To make the public more aware of some of the great applications, I started a mathematical investigation on the use of polyethene inside paper coffee cups and had my paper published by the STEM Fellowship Journal (SFJ).
My paper proposes a mathematical strategy that uses differential and integral calculus to determine the cup dimensions that minimize the amount of polyethene needed to coat the internal surface area of a coffee cup while still keeping the cup’s volume and lid size the same.
Feel free to check out the paper here.

Math Publication - A Comparative Analysis of the Gini Index

February 2021 - This paper, again published by SFJ, was another one of my attempts at highlighting the beauty of math. The focus of this paper was on the Gini Index, which summarizes the income disparity of an area into a single value that falls between zero and one. There are numerous methods for evaluating the Gini index, and my paper compares the Gini indices obtained using three of the earliest developed methods to see whether there would be any discrepancies between the obtained results.
Feel free to check out the paper here.

Python Program - OnTrack

May 2020 - My first introduction to the world of CS was through Stanford University’s Code in Place, an intro-to-coding program designed during the time of COVID-19 that is equivalent to Stanford’s CS106A. The two month long program explained introductory coding concepts using Python in PyCharm. Although the program was offered during the start of the pandemic, you can check out Stanford's website to see whether they plan to offer it again in the future.
In order to apply all the coding skills I had gained, I decided to build OnTrack. OnTrack is an interactive Python program that allows the user to keep track of their food consumption, physical activity, and water intake with the ultimate hope of encouraging users to be more mindful of their health.
