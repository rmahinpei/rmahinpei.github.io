Programming is an ever-growing field. New languages and technologies are always being developed, so it's important to always keep learning regardless of your level. Here are some of my personal favourite resources, ranging from online courses to YouTube channels.

~WEBSITES~

Microsoft Learn Docs

  • A compilation of learning modules for varying levels of expertise.
  • Highly recommended if you want to learn more about Microsoft technologies.

freeCodeCamp

  • A compilation of learning modules for varying levels of expertise.
  • Have modules that teach programming languages as well as important computer science concepts.

CS50x

  • Harvard's free introductory computer science course covering important concepts as well as C, Python, SQL, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
  • I personally find the delivery and organiztion of this course to be phenomenal!

edX & Coursera

  • Two platforms with free courses covering a variety of topics, including computer science and programming.
  • I personally took most of Microsoft's edX courses and would highly recommend them.
  • Although there are many great courses on Coursera, I would highly recommend University of Princeton's Programming with a Purpose for anyone who's new to the field.

~YOUTUBE CHANNELS~

freeCodeCamp

  • Just like with their website, their YouTube channel covers a variety of programming topics through in-depth videos.
  • Their videos are often made by other programming-focused YouTubers, so it's a great way to find other YouTube channels for learning.

Tech with Tim

  • Great YouTuber with insightful videos for beginner and intermediate learners.
  • Videos not only cover languages and programming concepts but also general advice and tips for those new to the field.
